Sporting Events


Athletics

Athletics, the oldest sport of which we have any records, is part of the Olympic Games ever since the 1896 Games in Athens and features a variety of events, making it the event with the highest number of participants in the Olympic Games. Athletics takes place in 3 zones : roads (for the marathon and walking), the track and trackside. And today, athletics is considered as the king of sports in the Summer Olympics. A total of 48 events : 23 men’s events, 23 women’s events and 2 mixed events

  • Hotel de ville (marathon) : located in the 4th arrondissement of Paris
  • Invalides (marathon) : located in the 7th arrondissement of Paris
  • Stade de France
  • Trocadéro (marathon et 20 km marche) : located in the 16th arrondissement of Paris

Swimming

Swimming, which dates back to prehistoric times, has been competitive since the 19th century, with a multitude of events based on 4 strokes: breaststroke, butterfly, backstroke and freestyle. Today, swimming is one of the sports with the most events at the Olympic Games, and also one of the most popular. 35 events in total: 17 men's events, 17 women's events and 1 mixed event Swimming takes place on a single location :

  • Paris La Défense Arena : located in Nanterre

Boccia (Paralympic)

Boccia is a little-known sport at the Paralympic Games, first introduced in 1984 and practiced by athletes with severe physical disabilities. Today, this unique sport underlines the inclusion of athletes with disabilities and the increased visibility of Paralympic sports. 11 events in total: 4 men's events, 4 women's events and 3 mixed events Boccia takes place on a single location:

  • Arena Paris Sud :located in the 15th arrondissement of Paris

How were the facilities at the Olympic sports locations (including these 3 sports) designed to function during the Olympic and Paralympic Games? :

The facilities were designed according to various criteria: Environmental preservation : Preserve traffic routes as much as possible, as well as the access to public spaces and services. Respect of the protection measures implemented in collaboration with the ABF (Architectes des Bâtiments de France). These measures are designed to preserve the unique features of our historic monuments and sites Most of the Olympic and Paralympic events' locations are far from French heritage sites, to limit the risk of deterioration. For event locations that are close to heritage sites, heritage sites potentially at risk of damage from the Olympic and Paralympic Games (vegetated areas, buildings, etc.) have been identified and appropriate protection measures have been put in place (wooden protections, barriers, etc.). Economic preservation : Limit the negative impact of the event locations on various businesses and companies. Social preservation : Limit noise pollution from the event sites for residents and local residents Large number of nearby public transport services and short journey times by public transport Ability to accommodate large numbers of spectators
